SECTION: IPC 420
CATEGORY: Fraud / Cybercrime

Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property —

Whoever cheats and thereby dishonestly induces the person deceived to deliver any property to any person, or to make, alter or destroy the whole or any part of a valuable security, or anything which is signed or sealed, and which is capable of being converted into a valuable security, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IPC 406
CATEGORY: Criminal Breach of Trust

Punishment for criminal breach of trust.

Whoever commits criminal breach of trust shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IPC 354
CATEGORY: Women Safety

Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty.

Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than one year but which may extend to five years, and shall also be liable to fine.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IPC 415
CATEGORY: Fraud / Cybercrime

Cheating.

Whoever, by deceiving any person, fraudulently or dishonestly induces the person so deceived to deliver any property to any person, or to consent that any person shall retain any property, or intentionally induces the person so deceived to do or omit to do anything which he would not do or omit if he were not so deceived, and which act or omission causes or is likely to cause damage or harm to that person in body, mind, reputation or property, is said to cheat.

Explanation:
A dishonest concealment of facts is a deception within the meaning of this section.

Examples:
• Pretending to be a government officer to obtain goods.
• Selling fake branded items by deception.
• Showing false samples to induce purchase.
• Taking money without intention to repay.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IT ACT 66C
CATEGORY: Cybercrime

Punishment for identity theft.

Whoever fraudulently uses another person's electronic signature, password, or unique identification feature shall be punished with imprisonment up to three years and fine up to one lakh rupees.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IT ACT 66D
CATEGORY: Cybercrime

Cheating by personation using computer resources.

Whoever cheats by impersonating another person using communication devices or computer resources shall be punished with imprisonment up to three years and fine up to one lakh rupees.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IPC 509
CATEGORY: Women Safety

Word, gesture or act intended to insult the modesty of a woman.

Whoever insults the modesty of a woman through words, gestures, sounds, or invasion of privacy shall be punished with imprisonment up to three years and fine.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IPC 354A
CATEGORY: Women Safety

Sexual harassment.

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome physical contact, demands for sexual favors, showing pornography against a woman's will, or sexually colored remarks. Punishment may extend to three years imprisonment or fine or both.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: CPA SECTION 12
CATEGORY: Consumer Rights

How a consumer complaint can be filed.

A consumer, consumer association, group of consumers, or government authority may file a complaint before a District Consumer Forum regarding defective goods or deficient services.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IPC 506
CATEGORY: Public Safety

Criminal intimidation.

Whoever threatens another person causing fear of injury to body, reputation, or property shall be punished with imprisonment, or fine, or both.
--------------------------------------------------
SECTION: IPC 468
CATEGORY: Fraud

Forgery for purpose of cheating.

Whoever creates a forged document intending it to be used for cheating shall be punished with imprisonment up to seven years and fine.
